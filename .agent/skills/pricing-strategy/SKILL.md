### Pricing Fundamentals
- **The Three Pricing Axes**:
  - **Packaging**: What features, limits, and support levels are in each tier.
  - **Pricing Metric**: What you charge for (per user, usage, flat fee).
  - **Price Point**: The actual dollar amounts.
- **Value-Based Pricing**:
  - **Perceived Value**: The ceiling (maximum customer is willing to pay).
  - **Price Point**: Should be between the ceiling and the floor.
  - **Next Best Alternative**: The floor for differentiation.
  - **Cost to Serve**: Only a baseline, not the basis for price.

### Value Metrics
- **What is it?**: What you charge for. It should scale with value received.
- **Good Metric Characteristics**:
  - Aligns price with value delivered.
  - Easy to understand.
  - Scales as the customer grows.
  - Hard to game/manipulate.
- **Selection Rule**: "As a customer uses more of [metric], do they get more value?" If yes, it's a good metric.

### Tier Structure (Good-Better-Best)
- **Good (Entry)**: Core features, limited usage, low price.
- **Better (Recommended)**: Full features, reasonable limits, anchor price.
- **Best (Premium)**: Everything, advanced features, 2-3x Better price.
- **Differentiation Methods**:
  - **Feature Gating**: Basic vs. advanced features.
  - **Usage Limits**: Same features, different volume.
  - **Support Level**: Email vs. Priority vs. Dedicated.
  - **Access**: API, SSO, custom branding.

### Pricing Page Best Practices
- **Above the Fold**:
  - Clear tier comparison table.
  - Highlight the "Recommended" tier.
  - Monthly/annual billing toggle.
  - Primary CTA for each tier.
- **Core Elements**:
  - Feature comparison table.
  - Persona-based descriptions ("Who it's for").
  - FAQ section.
  - Annual discount callout (17-20%).
  - Trust signals (logos, guarantees).
- **Pricing Psychology**:
  - **Anchoring**: Show the highest-priced option first.
  - **Decoy Effect**: Make the middle tier look like the best value.
  - **Charm Pricing**: Use $49 for value; $50 for premium (round pricing).

### Pricing Research & Execution
- **Research Methods**:
  - **Van Westendorp**: For price sensitivity.
  - **MaxDiff**: For feature preference.
- **When to Raise Prices**:
  - High NPS/CSAT scores.
  - Significant value additions recently.
  - Prices haven't changed in 2+ years.
  - Profit margins are shrinking.

### Task-Specific Questions

2. What's your current ARPU and conversion rate?
3. What's your primary value metric?
4. Who are your main pricing personas?
5. Are you self-serve, sales-led, or hybrid?
6. What pricing changes are you considering?
