### Foundational Thinking Models
- **First Principles**: Break problems down to basic truths. Ask "why" repeatedly. Don't just copy competitors.
- **Jobs to Be Done**: People "hire" products for outcomes. Focus on the job, not features (drill vs. hole).
- **Circle of Competence**: Focus on what you're good at. Venture outside only with expert help.
- **Inversion**: Ask "What would guarantee failure?" and avoid those things.
- **Occam's Razor**: The simplest explanation is usually correct. Check the obvious first.
- **Pareto Principle (80/20 Rule)**: 80% of results come from 20% of efforts. Focus on the vital few.
- **Local vs. Global Optima**: Don't get stuck optimizing a small part (local) if the whole strategy (global) is wrong.
- **Theory of Constraints**: Find and fix the single bottleneck limiting your system.
- **Opportunity Cost**: Every choice has a cost of what you gave up. Compare against alternatives.
- **Law of Diminishing Returns**: Additional investment yields smaller gains after a certain point.
- **Second-Order Thinking**: Consider the effects of the effects (e.g., flash sales train customers to wait for discounts).
- **Map ≠ Territory**: Models (personas, data) represent reality but aren't reality. Stay in touch with real users.
- **Probabilistic Thinking**: Think in likelihoods, not certainties. Plan for multiple outcomes.
- **Barbell Strategy**: 80% safety (proven channels), 20% high-risk/high-reward (experimental bets).

### Understanding Buyers & Human Psychology
- **Fundamental Attribution Error**: Attribute failures to character, not circumstances. Fix the process first.
- **Mere Exposure Effect**: People prefer what they've seen before. Consistent presence builds trust.
- **Availability Heuristic**: Vivid examples (case studies) make success feel more common and achievable.
- **Confirmation Bias**: People seek info that confirms existing beliefs. Align messaging with audience beliefs.
- **The Lindy Effect**: Reliable principles outlast trendy tactics. Don't abandon fundamentals for fads.
- **Mimetic Desire**: People want what others want. Use waitlists, exclusivity, and social proof.
- **Sunk Cost Fallacy**: Don't justify future spend on underperforming campaigns based on past spend.
- **Endowment Effect**: People value what they "own" more. Use free trials and samples.
- **IKEA Effect**: People value what they helped build. Use customization and configuration.
- **Zero-Price Effect**: "Free" is psychologically powerful. The jump from $1 to $0 is bigger than $2 to $1.
- **Hyperbolic Discounting**: People prefer immediate rewards. Emphasize "Start today" over "ROI in 6 months."
- **Status-Quo Bias**: People prefer current states. Reduce friction to switch and make it feel safe.
- **Default Effect**: People accept pre-selected options. Pre-select the preferred plan (opt-out vs. opt-in).
- **Paradox of Choice**: Too many options paralyze. Limit choices (3 tiers instead of 7).
- **Goal-Gradient Effect**: People work harder as they get closer to a goal. Use progress bars.
- **Peak-End Rule**: Experiences are judged by the peak and the end. Design memorable moments and endings.
- **Zeigarnik Effect**: Unfinished tasks occupy the mind. Use "80% done" to pull people to finish.
- **Pratfall Effect**: Showing a small flaw makes competent people/brands more likable and trustworthy.

### Influencing Behavior & Persuasion
- **Reciprocity Principle**: Give value first (content, tools) to create an obligation to return the favor.
- **Commitment & Consistency**: Get small commitments first (email signups) to build toward larger ones.
- **Authority Bias**: Use expert endorsements, certifications, and "featured in" logos to build trust.
- **Liking / Similarity Bias**: Use relatable spokespeople and "for X by X" messaging.
- **Unity Principle**: Shared identity drives influence. Position brand as part of the customer's tribe.
- **Scarcity / Urgency**: Limited availability/time increases perceived value. Use only when genuine.
- **Foot-in-the-Door**: Start with a small request, then escalate (Trial → Paid → Enterprise).
- **Door-in-the-Face**: Start with a large request, then retreat to the target one to make it feel reasonable.
- **Loss Aversion**: People work harder to avoid loss than to gain. "Don't miss out" > "You could gain."
- **Anchoring Effect**: The first number seen influences judgment. Show higher prices first.
- **Decoy Effect**: Add an inferior third option to make one of the original two look better.
- **Framing Effect**: Present identical facts differently to change perception (90% success vs. 10% failure).
- **Contrast Effect**: Show a clear "before" state to make the "after" improvement vivid.

### Pricing Psychology
- **Charm Pricing**: $99 feels much cheaper than $100. Use .99 for value-focused products.
- **Rounded-Price Effect**: Round numbers ($100) signal premium quality; charm prices signal value.
- **Rule of 100**: Under $100: use % discounts. Over $100: use absolute ($) discounts.
- **Price Relativity**: Three tiers (Good-Better-Best) where the middle is the target.
- **Mental Accounting**: Reframe costs ("$1/day" feels cheaper than "$30/month").

### Design & Delivery Models
- **Hick's Law**: Decision time increases with choice complexity. Simplify CTAs and forms.
- **AIDA Funnel**: Attention → Interest → Desire → Action. Structure pages to move through these stages.
- **Rule of 7**: Prospects need ~7 touchpoints. Use multi-touch campaigns and retargeting.
- **Nudge Theory**: Small changes in presentation (defaults, ordering) guide behavior.
- **BJ Fogg Behavior Model**: Behavior = Motivation × Ability × Prompt. All three must be present.
- **EAST Framework**: Make behaviors Easy, Attractive, Social, and Timely.
- **COM-B Model**: Behavior requires Capability, Opportunity, and Motivation.
- **Activation Energy**: Reduce starting friction (pre-fill forms, templates) to make the first step easy.
- **North Star Metric**: Identify one core metric capturing value and align efforts toward it.
- **The Cobra Effect**: Be careful—incentives can backfire and produce opposite results.

### Growth & Scaling Models
- **Feedback Loops**: Build virtuous cycles (Users → Content → SEO → Users).
- **Compounding**: Small, consistent gains accumulate exponentially. Start early.
- **Network Effects**: Products become more valuable as more people use them.
- **Flywheel Effect**: Sustained effort creates momentum that maintains itself.
- **Switching Costs**: Increase retention by ethically increasing costs to change (integrations, data).
- **Exploration vs. Exploitation**: Balance trying new things with optimizing what works.
- **Critical Mass**: Reach a tipping point in one segment before expanding.
- **Survivorship Bias**: Study failed campaigns as well as successes to avoid false conclusions.

## Task-Specific Questions
1. What specific behavior are you trying to influence?
2. What does your customer believe before encountering your marketing?
3. Where in the journey (awareness → consideration → decision) is this?
4. What's currently preventing the desired action?
5. Have you tested this with real customers?
